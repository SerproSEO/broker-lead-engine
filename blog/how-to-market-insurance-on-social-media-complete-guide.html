FINRA and Regulatory Compliance

Insurance social media marketing must comply with FINRA regulations and state insurance requirements. Non-compliance can result in fines, license suspension, and legal issues.

⚠️ Compliance Alert

4 new state privacy laws activated in 2024, with 7 more enacted and 5 new privacy laws effective in 2025. Stay current with evolving regulations to avoid penalties.

FINRA social media requirements

FINRA divides social media communications into two categories with different compliance requirements:

Static content (requires pre-approval)

  • Blog posts and website content
  • Profile information and company descriptions
  • Prepared posts scheduled in advance
  • Marketing materials shared on social media
  • Must be approved by registered principal before posting

Interactive content (subject to supervision)

  • Real-time posts and responses
  • Comments on customer posts
  • Live streaming and video calls
  • Spontaneous social media interactions
  • Subject to supervisory standards but no pre-approval required

Key compliance requirements

  • Record retention: All business-related communications must be archived for 3+ years
  • Fair and balanced content: All communications must be "fair, balanced and complete"
  • Suitability standards: Any recommendations must follow suitability rules
  • Third-party content: "Liking" or "sharing" makes firms responsible under all communication rules
  • Supervision policies: Required written policies for all business social media use
  • Principal approval: Registered principal must pre-approve recommendations

Compliance best practices

  • Implement message approval workflows for regulated content
  • Use social media management tools with archiving capabilities
  • Train all staff on FINRA social media requirements
  • Maintain separate personal and business social media accounts
  • Document all approval processes and supervisory reviews
  • Regular compliance audits of social media activities

Frequently Asked Questions About Insurance Social Media Marketing

Which social media platform delivers the best ROI for insurance agencies?

Facebook delivers the highest ROI with 28% of marketers citing it as their top performer. It offers the largest audience reach (123K median followers for insurance brands) and effective lead generation tools, despite higher CPC costs ($4.57) for insurance keywords.

Do I need pre-approval for all social media posts as an insurance agent?

Only static content requires pre-approval from a registered principal under FINRA rules. This includes prepared posts, marketing materials, and profile information. Interactive content like real-time responses and comments are subject to supervision but don't need pre-approval.

What type of content performs best for insurance on social media?

Short-form video content (under 60 seconds) delivers 50% engagement rates compared to 17% for longer content. Educational content, customer testimonials, and behind-the-scenes content perform exceptionally well, with 93% of video marketers reporting positive ROI.

How much should I budget for insurance social media advertising?

Budget 20-30% above general business advertising due to insurance keyword premiums. Expect $4.57 CPC on Facebook, $5.39 on LinkedIn, but lower costs on TikTok ($0.31-$0.66). Allocate budget based on audience: 50% Facebook, 25% Instagram, 15% LinkedIn, 10% TikTok.

Can I use customer testimonials in my social media marketing?

Yes, customer testimonials are highly effective (high conversion rates) and permissible when properly disclosed. Ensure you have written consent, follow state privacy laws, and maintain FINRA compliance for any recommendations. Video testimonials perform best across all platforms.
