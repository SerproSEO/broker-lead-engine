Assumptive Closing Phrases:

  • "When we get this set up..." (not "if you decide to buy")
  • "The next step is..." (implies they're moving forward)
  • "I'll get this started today..." (assumes they're ready)
  • "Your first payment will be..." (discusses implementation)
  • "You'll receive your policy documents..." (post-sale focus)

Implementation Process:

  1. Build Agreement: Get multiple small "yes" responses
  2. Summarize Benefits: Recap value and protection provided
  3. Handle Concerns: Address any hesitations naturally
  4. Assume the Sale: Discuss next steps as if they've decided
  5. Complete Application: Start paperwork while momentum is high
Assumptive Close Example: "Based on everything we've discussed, the $500,000 term life policy with the disability rider gives you exactly the protection your family needs. Let me get this application started so we can have your coverage in place by next week. I'll need your Social Security number for the application, and we can set up the automatic payment from your checking account to make sure there's never a lapse in coverage. Which checking account would you prefer to use for the monthly payments?"

Key Point: Use assumptive closing after you've built sufficient value and addressed their concerns, not as a pressure tactic.

The HEAR Method:

  • Halt: Stop talking and listen completely
  • Empathize: Acknowledge their concern as valid
  • Analyze: Ask questions to understand the real issue
  • Respond: Provide a tailored solution or clarification

Common Objections and Proven Responses

Objection: "I need to think about it"

Response: "I completely understand - this is an important decision. Can I ask what specifically you'd like to think about? Is it the coverage options, the price, or something else? Because if I can clarify that for you now, it might save you some time."

Follow-up: "What information would help you feel confident about moving forward?"

Objection: "I want to shop around"

Response: "That's smart - you should compare options. I actually encourage my clients to do that. Can I ask what you'll be comparing? Because I can show you how our coverage stacks up against the top 3 competitors right now, which might save you some time."

Follow-up: "What would another agent have to offer to make you switch from what we've put together?"

Objection: "It's too expensive"

Response: "I understand cost is important. When you say it's too expensive, are you comparing it to your current coverage, or is it more than you budgeted? Because we might be able to adjust the coverage to better fit your budget while still protecting what's most important to you."

Follow-up: "What would be a comfortable monthly investment for this protection?"

Objection: "I have to talk to my spouse"

Response: "Of course - this affects both of you. Would it be helpful if I spoke with both of you together? I can explain the options and answer any questions they might have. When would be a good time for a brief call with both of you?"

Follow-up: "What concerns do you think they'll have that I should be prepared to address?"
